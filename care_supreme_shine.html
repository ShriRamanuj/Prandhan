Care Supreme Shine · What You Must Know
Waiting periods — complete guide
Shine's waiting periods are identical to Care Supreme. The co-payment, sub-limits and room rent restriction are the operational differences — not the waiting period structure.
Initial waiting period
30 days
No illness claims in the first 30 days. Accidents are always exempt. Waived if you have prior continuous coverage of 12+ months.
Named ailments / specific diseases
24 months
Listed conditions — cataract, hernia, gallstones, joint replacement, BPH and more — excluded for 2 years. Portability credit reduces this. Accidents always exempt.
Pre-existing diseases (PED)
36 months
All pre-existing conditions excluded for 3 years. Reducible to 1 or 2 years via optional PED Wait Period Modification add-on. Portability credit also applies independently.
Instant Cover add-on
30 days only
Optional add-on: BP, Diabetes, Hyperlipidemia and Asthma covered after just 30 days. Under Care Advanced: additionally covers COPD, Obesity and CAD post-PTCA.
Co-payment (not a waiting period)
20% forever
20% co-payment on every admissible claim — mandatory, permanent, from Day 1. Cannot be waived. The key structural difference from standard Care Supreme.
Moratorium period
60 months
After 5 continuous years of coverage, no claim can be contested on grounds of non-disclosure — except proven fraud. Strongest long-term protection.
Portability credit
Carry forward
Waiting periods served with your previous insurer are credited here under IRDAI portability rules. Apply at least 30 days before your current policy renewal date.
Room rent restriction
Always applies
Most Economical Single Private Room only. If a higher room is taken, proportionate deduction applies on all associated charges — surgeon, nursing, OT etc. Plan your hospitalisation accordingly.
✨ When is Care Supreme Shine the right choice?
Shine works best as a starting point — when the premium of standard Care Supreme is beyond the current budget, but the family wants genuine hospitalisation coverage rather than no cover at all.

Ideal profiles for Shine: Young earners buying their first policy, families supplementing corporate cover, individuals with a personal health corpus who can self-fund the 20% co-payment, and those who plan to upgrade to full Care Supreme in 1–2 years as income grows.

When to choose standard Care Supreme instead: If you can afford the premium difference, the CB growth (50%/year, never reduces), unlimited auto recharge for the same illness, zero co-payment and no sub-limits make Care Supreme significantly more powerful over a 3–5 year horizon.
Room rent sub-limit — plan your hospitalisation
The Most Economical Single Private Room restriction is the most practically impactful feature of Shine. In a good private hospital, the economical single room may be significantly lower than the preferred room. If your actual room charges exceed the allowed category, ALL associated charges — surgeon, anaesthetist, nursing, OT, medicines — are proportionately reduced. When admitted to a network hospital, always confirm which room category falls within the Shine entitlement before checking in.
