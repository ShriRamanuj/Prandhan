Senior Health Advantage · What You Must Know
Waiting periods — complete senior guide
Senior Health Advantage has a 24-month PED wait as an optional cover — significantly better than many senior plans. Here is every waiting period and restriction clearly explained.
Initial waiting period
30 days
No illness claims in the first 30 days. Accidents are always exempt. Waived on portability if prior continuous coverage exceeds 12 months.
Named ailments wait period
24 months
Specific listed conditions — cataract, hernia, joint replacement, BPH, gallstones, kidney stones and more — excluded for 2 years. Portability credit reduces this.
Pre-existing diseases (PED)
24 months*
PED wait is an optional cover (additional premium). With it, all pre-existing conditions are covered after 24 months. Without this optional cover, PED may remain excluded permanently — ask your advisor.
Co-payment (age ≤70)
50% forever
50% co-payment is mandatory and permanent for insured up to age 70. This is an optional cover that must be opted. It applies from Day 1, every claim, throughout the policy.
Co-payment (age >70)
60% forever
An additional 10% co-payment applies once the insured crosses age 70. Total co-payment becomes 60%. This additional 10% is also an optional cover. Cannot be waived.
Annual health check-up
Available Day 1
Health check-up available from Year 1 itself — no waiting period. CBC, urine, FBS, lipid profile, KFT, ECG covered cashless at network labs on advance booking.
Moratorium period
60 months
After 5 continuous years of coverage, no claim can be contested on grounds of non-disclosure — except proven fraud. The most important long-term protection for seniors.
Portability credit
Carry forward
Waiting periods served with a previous insurer are credited here under IRDAI portability rules. Apply at least 30 days before current policy renewal. NCB may also transfer.
🧓 Key insight for families insuring elderly parents
Why Senior Health Advantage despite the 50–60% co-payment?

Most standard health plans will either refuse seniors above 65–70, or impose very high loading on premium, or exclude all pre-existing conditions permanently. Senior Health Advantage accepts seniors with no upper age limit and covers PED after just 24 months (optional cover).

The co-payment is not a punishment — it is the mechanism that makes premium affordable. A 50% insurer contribution on a ₹5L hospitalisation still means ₹2.5L relief. Without any policy, the family pays 100% themselves.

Recommendation: Pair Senior Health Advantage with a personal health corpus of ₹1–2L for your co-payment portion. The insurer covers the rest. Your Sonis advisor can help structure this appropriately.
Optional covers — understand what you are buying
Several benefits marked with (*) in this plan are optional covers that must be specifically opted and paid for. These include: PED wait period cover, NCB, co-payment (yes — even the co-payment option must be opted), room rent, ICU charges, advance technology methods and pre/post-hospitalisation. Confirm with your Sonis advisor exactly which optional covers are included in the plan you are buying so there is no gap in coverage at claim time.
