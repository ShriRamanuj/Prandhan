Care Heart · What You Must Know Before Buying
Waiting periods — the heart patient's guide
Care Heart's 2-year PED waiting period is dramatically shorter than the standard 36 months — its biggest advantage for cardiac patients. Here is every waiting period explained clearly.
♥ The most important fact about Care Heart
Standard health plans impose a 36-month (3-year) waiting period for pre-existing diseases including cardiac conditions. This means if you have had a heart attack, bypass surgery or angioplasty, a normal health policy would not cover cardiac-related hospitalisations for 3 years after purchase.

Care Heart cuts this to just 24 months (2 years) — the single biggest advantage of this specialist plan. If you have a cardiac event in Year 3 or later, it is fully covered (subject to co-payment and applicable sub-limits).
Initial waiting period
30 days
No illness claims in the first 30 days. Accidents are always exempt. Waived if you have prior continuous coverage of 12+ months under another health policy.
Pre-existing diseases (PED)
24 months
All pre-existing conditions — including the cardiac condition — are excluded for only 2 years. This is the plan's defining advantage vs standard 36-month PED in other policies.
Specific disease waiting period
24 months
Named ailments — cataract, hernia, gallstones, joint replacement, BPH and more — are excluded for 2 years. Same as PED period. Accidents are always exempt.
Cardiac health check-up
Available Year 1
Annual cardiac health check-up is available from Year 1 itself — once per insured per policy year on cashless basis at Care's network providers. No waiting period on this benefit.
Moratorium period
60 months
After 5 continuous years of coverage, no claim can be contested by the insurer on grounds of non-disclosure — except proven fraud. Your strongest long-term legal protection.
Portability credit
Carry forward
Waiting periods served under another health insurer can be credited here under IRDAI portability norms. Apply at least 30 days before your current policy renewal date.
Co-payment (not a waiting period)
From Day 1
20% co-payment (entry age ≤70) or 30% (entry age >70) applies on every admissible claim from Day 1, forever. This is mandatory and cannot be waived.
Home care (optional add-on)
No extra wait
If opted, Home Care add-on follows the same waiting periods as the base plan. The deductible of 1 day applies per occurrence — Days 2 to 7 per occurrence are covered at ₹1,000–₹2,000 per day.
♥ Comparison — Care Heart vs a standard health plan for a cardiac patient
Standard health plan PED wait: 36 months (3 years) — cardiac events in Year 1, 2 and 3 are not covered.
Care Heart PED wait: 24 months (2 years) — cardiac events in Year 3 onwards are covered (subject to co-pay).

This 12-month difference is extremely significant for a cardiac patient. The probability of a cardiac-related hospitalisation in Year 3 after surgery is real — and Care Heart is the only plan that covers it at the 2-year mark instead of the 3-year mark.

Trade-off: The mandatory co-payment (20%/30%) is the cost of this early coverage. For a ₹5L claim at 20% co-pay, you pay ₹1L and the insurer pays ₹4L — still far better than paying the entire ₹5L yourself.
Plan your co-payment budget
Unlike standard plans, Care Heart's co-payment is mandatory and permanent. We recommend budgeting for 20–30% of a likely claim amount as your own contribution. For cardiac surgeries averaging ₹3–8L, your share would be ₹60,000–₹2.4L depending on your age. Having a personal health corpus or top-up policy alongside Care Heart is a sound strategy.
