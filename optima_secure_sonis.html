Waiting periods — know before you buy
Every health policy has time periods during which certain conditions are not covered. Understanding these upfront prevents claim surprises later.
Initial waiting
30 days
No illness claims in the first 30 days from inception. Accidents are always exempt. Waived automatically if you have continuous prior coverage of 12+ months.
Pre-existing diseases (PED)
36 months
Any disease diagnosed before policy start is excluded for 3 years. Reducible to 24 months or 12 months via the optional PED Wait Period Modification add-on (channel-level only — ask your agent).
Specified diseases
24 months
40+ listed conditions including cataract, hernia, gallstones, kidney stones, joint replacement, varicose veins, hysterectomy, benign tumours and more are excluded for 2 years. Accidents are exempt.
ABCD Chronic Care (add-on)
30 days only
With the ABCD Chronic Care paid add-on, hospitalisation due to Asthma, Blood Pressure, Cholesterol and Diabetes is covered after just 30 days — instead of the usual 2–3 year PED wait. The single biggest waiting-period advantage in this product.
Maternity (Parenthood add-on)
24 months
Maternity expenses, IVF treatments and embryo storage are covered after 24 months from when the Parenthood add-on is first purchased. Must be opted at inception — cannot be added mid-policy.
Moratorium period
60 months
After 5 continuous years of coverage (including portability credit), no claim can be contested by the insurer on grounds of non-disclosure or misrepresentation — except proven fraud. This is your strongest long-term legal protection.
Portability credit
Carry forward
Years served with your previous insurer count towards all waiting periods. Port at least 30 days before renewal. If you have served 2 years elsewhere, only 1 year of PED waiting remains here.
Global cover (planned treatment)
Restart on port
All waiting periods restart from scratch for planned overseas treatments if you port or migrate into the Global or Global Plus plan. Emergency overseas treatments are not subject to this restart.
Important: always disclose pre-existing conditions honestly
Non-disclosure of pre-existing diseases can lead to policy cancellation or full claim rejection. However, under IRDAI guidelines, the insurer may instead offer to continue coverage by (a) permanently excluding the undisclosed condition, (b) adding an additional 3-year waiting period for that condition, or (c) levying an underwriting loading — all subject to your acceptance. After the 60-month moratorium, even undisclosed conditions are protected except for proven fraud.
