Care Supreme · What You Must Know Before Buying
Waiting periods — complete guide
Every health policy has time periods during which certain conditions are not covered. Understanding these prevents claim surprises later.
Initial waiting period
30 days
No illness claims in the first 30 days from policy start. Accidents are always exempt. Waived if you have 12+ months of prior continuous coverage.
Named ailment / specified diseases
24 months
Listed conditions — cataract, hernia, gallstones, joint replacement, varicose veins and more — excluded for 2 years. Portability credit reduces this. Accidents always exempt.
Pre-existing diseases (PED)
36 months
Any condition diagnosed before policy start excluded for 3 years. Reducible to 1 or 2 years via optional PED Wait Period Modification add-on. Portability credit also applies.
Instant Cover (base add-on)
30 days only
Optional add-on: BP, Diabetes, Hyperlipidemia and Asthma covered after just 30 days — replacing the standard 36-month PED wait for these 4 conditions specifically.
Instant Cover (Care Advanced)
30 days only
Under Care Advanced add-on: additionally covers COPD, Obesity and Coronary Artery Disease (with PTCA done at least 1 year earlier) — all after just 30 days.
Moratorium period
60 months
After 5 continuous years, no claim can be contested on grounds of non-disclosure — except proven fraud. Your strongest long-term legal protection.
Portability credit
Carry forward
Waiting periods served with your previous insurer count here under IRDAI portability norms. Apply at least 30 days before your renewal date.
PED wait modification (add-on)
1 or 2 years
Reduces PED wait from 36 months to 2 years or 1 year on payment of additional premium. Cannot be combined with Instant Cover — choose one or the other.
Always disclose pre-existing conditions honestly
Non-disclosure can lead to claim rejection or policy cancellation. Under IRDAI guidelines the insurer may instead offer to permanently exclude the condition, add a waiting period, or apply a loading — all with your acceptance. After 60 months continuous cover, even undisclosed conditions are protected except for fraud. Your Sonis advisor will guide you on the best declaration approach for your health history.
